1. Gyeongbokgung Palace, Seoul

Gyeongbokgung Palace was the main royal palace of Joseon Dynasty. Gyeongbokgung Palace is arguably the most beautiful, and remains the largest of all five palaces. Walk around the palace and enjoy the wonderful sight.

  • Opened hours: 09:00 - 18:00, Closed on Tuesdays
  • Price: ₩3,000 for age 25-64, Free for people wearing Hanbok
  • Public Transportation: Gyeongbokgung Sta.
  • Phone: 02-3700-3900

2. Myeongdong, Seoul

Myeongdong is Seoul’s shopping mecca and is a must-see for tourists, but it is not just a shopping destination; Myeongdong is also a hub of commerce, banking and culture with a daytime population of 1.5-2 million. For shoppers there is a mixture of street stalls and retail outlets selling everything from high fashion to casual attire, as well as many Korean cosmetics stores offering high-quality products at competitive prices.

  • Opened hours: Varies by Stores
  • Public Transportation: Myeong-dong Sta.
  • Phone: Varies by Stores

3. N Seoul Tower, Seoul

Namsan Seoul Tower was built in 1969 as Korea's first integrated transmission tower beaming television and radio broadcasts across the capital. Since opening to the public in 1980, it has become a much-loved Seoul landmark. 

  • Opened Hours: 10:00 - 23:00
  • Price: ₩11,000
  • Public Transportation: Bus 02 or Bus 04

4. Lotte World & Lotte World Tower, Seoul

Lotte World is a major recreation complex in Seoul, South Korea. It consists of the world's largest indoor theme park, an outdoor amusement park called "Magic Island", an artificial island inside a lake linked by monorail, shopping malls, a luxury hotel, a Korean folk museum, sports facilities, and movie theaters. Lotte World Tower is a 556-metre, 123-story skyscraper located nearby.

  • Opened Hours: 10:00 - 21:00
  • Price: ₩56,000 for adult
  • Public Transportation: Jamsil Sta.
  • Phone: 1661-2000

5. Korean Folk Village, Yongin-si, Gyeonggi-do

Korean Folk Village introduces traditional culture from the late Joseon period to both local and international visitors through cultural classes experience, shaman faith, seasonal customs and others. 

  • Opened Hours: 10:00 - 19:00
  • Price: ₩22,000 for adult
  • Public Transportation: Bus 5001-1 at Sinnonhyeon Sta., Gangnam Sta., Yangjae Sta., or Yangjae Citizen's Forest Sta.
  • Phone: 031-288-0000

6. Haeundae, Busan

Haeundae Beach is an urban beach in Busan, South Korea. Often dubbed one of the country's most famous and popular beaches, it is located in the eponymous Haeundae District.

  • Opened Dates: June - mid September
  • Phone: 051-749-7601

7. Everland, Yongin-si, Gyeonggi-do

Everland is South Korea's largest theme park. Located at the Everland Resort in Yongin, a city in Gyeonggi-do, it receives 5,85 million visitors annually and was ranked nineteenth in the world for amusement park attendance in 2018.

  • Opened Hours: 10:00 - 21:00
  • Price: ₩56,000 for adult
  • Public Transportation: Bus 5002 at Sinnonhyeon Sta., Gangnam Sta., Yangjae Sta., or Yangjae Citizen's Forest Sta.
  • Phone: 031-320-5000

8. Seongsan Ilchulbong, Jeju

Seongsan Ilchulbong Tuff Cone rose 180 meters above sea level due to a magma flow under the sea over 5,000 years ago. It was originally a separate island, but a build-up of sand and soil has connected it to the main island. It is one of the UNESCO World Heritages.

  • Opened Hours: 07:00 - 20:00
  • Price: ₩5,000 for adult
  • Phone: 064-783-0959

9. Jusangjeolli cliff, Jeju

Jusangjeoli cliff is a volcanic rock formation that many tourists are spotted to take a picture of the glorious view of this cliff. Jusangjeolli Cliff was formed due to the sudden cooling of lava from the Hallasan Mountain that bursts into the sea of Jungmun, thousands of years ago. It is one of the UNESCO World Heritages.

  • Opened Hours: 09:00 - 18:00
  • Phone: 064-738-1521

10. Seoraksan national park, Gangwon-do

Seoraksan National Park is a national park in South Korea. It listed by the South Korean government with UNESCO as a tentative World Heritage site.

  • Price: ₩11,000 for adult
  • Phone: 033-801-0900
